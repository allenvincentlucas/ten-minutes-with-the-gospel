How this works

Every entry here is a short, plain-language reflection on that day's Catholic Gospel reading — the kind of thing you can actually read in about ten minutes, with a homily, a reflection song, and a couple of Catechism cross-references if you want to go further. Nothing here replaces the Mass reading itself; every page links back to the official source.

New reflections get added over time. Once there's more than one, you'll find them organized by month in the archive.

Ten Minutes with the Gospel is an independent reflection project and is not an official publication of any diocese or parish. Scripture text on this site is the public-domain Douay-Rheims (Challoner) translation; the official Mass readings are linked from USCCB on each entry. Catechism references are paraphrased with links to the Church's own text. Videos and music are embedded from their original sources, never rehosted.
